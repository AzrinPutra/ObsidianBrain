# MARK213 - Marketing
## **MARK213: Marketing Principles – Comprehensive Notion Study Guide**
___
### **📘 About This Guide**
This guide consolidates **Lectures 1–9** of MARK213 (2026) into a structured, exam-ready resource. It includes **key concepts, Singapore-specific examples, learning points, key takeaways, and practice questions** to help you master marketing principles.
___
## **Lecture 1: Introduction to Marketing**
### **🔑 Key Concepts**
- **Marketing Definition**: The process of engaging customers and building profitable relationships through value creation and capture.
- **Needs, Wants, Demands**:
	- **Need**: Basic requirement (e.g., food).
	- **Want**: Culturally shaped desire (e.g., sushi).
	- **Demand**: Want + buying power (e.g., buying sushi).
- **Perceived Value = Benefits – Costs**.
- **Marketing Myopia**: Focusing on products, not customer needs (e.g., taxis vs Grab).
- **Market Offerings**: Goods, Services, Experiences (e.g., Jewel Changi).
- **Marketing Management Concepts**:
	- Production, Product, Selling, Marketing, Societal.
- **STP Framework**:
	- **Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning → Value Proposition**.
- **4Ps (Marketing Mix)**:
	- Product, Price, Place, Promotion.
- **Customer Relationships (CRM, CMR, CLV)**:
	- CRM: Managing interactions.
	- CMR: Customer-managed relationships.
	- CLV: Customer Lifetime Value.
- **Modern Marketing Landscape**: Digital, global, sustainable, not-for-profit.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **Marketing is value-driven, not product-driven**.
	- **Example**: Grab understood the need for *convenient, reliable mobility* while taxis focused on owning fleets and meters.
- **Avoid marketing myopia** by focusing on customer needs.
	- **Example**: SMRT initially focused on train operations, but commuters needed *reliable, predictable mobility experiences*.
- **STP is foundational** before 4Ps.
	- **Example**: Shopee segments by price-sensitive shoppers, targets them with deals, and positions as "cheaper, faster, fun shopping".
- **CRM → CMR → CLV** shows the evolution of customer relationships.
	- **Example**: Grab Diamond users receive personalized rewards (CRM), choose how to engage via app (CMR), and have high CLV.
___
### **🇸🇬 Singapore Examples**
- **Grab vs Public Transport**: Need (mobility), Want (convenience), Demand (Grab rides).
- **Mr Coconut**: Need (hydration), Want (trendy coconut drink), Demand (queuing 20 mins).
- **NTUC FairPrice**: Need (affordable food), Want (variety), Demand (FairPrice Xtra premium options).

## **Lecture 2: Marketing Environment**
### **🏢 Micro-Environment (Close Actors)**
- **Company** (Internal departments).
	- **Example**: NTUC FairPrice’s logistics department ensures daily fresh food replenishment.
- **Suppliers** (e.g., tapioca for bubble tea).
	- **Example**: Bubble tea shops affected by 2021 tapioca shortage.
- **Marketing Intermediaries** (e.g., Shopee Xpress, GrabFood).
- **Competitors** (Direct, Indirect, Substitutes).
	- **Example**: Grab vs Gojek (direct), MRT vs Taxi (indirect), Coffee vs Energy Drinks (substitutes).
- **Publics** (Media, Government, Financial, Local).
	- **Example**: NEA food safety inspections affect F&B reputation.
- **Customers** (Consumer, Business, Reseller, Government, International).
### **🌍 Macro-Environment (PESTLE)**
- **Political**: PDPA, NEA, MAS regulations.
	- **Example**: PDPA affects email marketing and loyalty programs.
- **Economic**: Inflation, income inequality, tourism.
	- **Example**: Inflation drives consumers to FairPrice house brands.
- **Social**: Aging population, experience-driven consumption, health trends.
	- **Example**: Silver Generation Office outreach for elderly products.
- **Technological**: High digital adoption, AI, automation.
	- **Example**: 98% smartphone penetration → mobile-first marketing.
- **Legal**: Employment laws, consumer protection.
- **Environmental**: Sustainability, Green Plan 2030.
	- **Example**: Plastic reduction → retailers offer reusable bags.
### **📊 SWOT Analysis**
- **Strengths/Weaknesses** (Internal).
- **Opportunities/Threats** (External from PESTLE).
	- **Example**: Eu Yan Sang SWOT—Strength: 140-year heritage; Weakness: perceived as old-fashioned.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **Micro vs Macro**: Micro = controllable, Macro = uncontrollable.
	- **Example**: Toast Box can choose suppliers (micro) but must comply with NEA regulations (macro).
- **PESTLE is systematic**—use it to structure macro analysis.
	- **Example**: Eu Yan Sang analyzed aging population (social) and digital health trends (technological).
- **SWOT is strategic**—links internal/external analysis to strategy.
- **Singapore-specific factors** (e.g., aging population, high digital adoption) must be integrated into analysis.
___
## **Lecture 3: Marketing Analytics, Research & AI**
### **📈 Marketing Information System (MIS)**
- Collects, processes, distributes marketing data.
- Components: Internal Data, Marketing Intelligence, Marketing Research.
	- **Example**: Sheng Siong uses real-time POS data to adjust procurement.
### **🔍 Marketing Research Process**
1. Define Problem (Exploratory, Descriptive, Causal).
	- **Example**: NTUC LearningHub explores declining enrolment via learner interviews.
2. Develop Research Plan (Primary vs Secondary Data).
3. Implement Research.
4. Interpret Results.
### **📊 Sampling Methods**
- Random, Stratified, Quota, Convenience.
	- **Example**: Stand outside a university for convenience sampling (skewed toward students).
### **🤖 AI & Analytics**
- **Types of Analytics**: Descriptive, Diagnostic, Predictive, Prescriptive.
	- **Example**: Grab uses predictive analytics to forecast demand by neighborhood.
- **AI in CRM**: Personalization, churn prediction, chatbots.
	- **Example**: SIA KrisFlyer uses AI for fare personalization and behavioral segmentation.
- **Ethics**: Bias, transparency, PDPA compliance.
	- **Example**: AI bias if pricing varies by device type (iPhone vs Android).
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **MIS turns data into decisions**.
	- **Example**: FairPrice uses Scan & Go data for personalized promotions.
- **Research must be systematic** and objective.
- **Sampling affects generalizability**—choose method based on goal.
- **AI enhances but does not replace human judgment**.
- **Ethics are non-negotiable** in data use.
	- **Example**: PDPA requires consent for data collection.
___
## **Lecture 4: Consumer & Business Buying Behaviour**
### **🛒 B2C: Consumer Behaviour**
- **Influencing Factors**:
	- Cultural, Social, Personal, Psychological, Situational.
	- **Example**: Halal certification (cultural), TikTok influencers (social), Buy Now Pay Later (psychological).
- **Consumer Decision Process**:
	- Need Recognition → Information Search → Evaluation → Purchase → Post-Purchase.
- **Singapore Consumer Types**:
	- Aspirational, Value-Driven, Experience-Seekers, etc.
### **🏢 B2B: Business Buying Behaviour**
- **Characteristics**: Fewer buyers, derived demand, complex process.
	- **Example**: NTUC LearningHub’s LMS demand depends on corporate training budgets (derived demand).
- **Buying Situations**: Straight Rebuy, Modified Rebuy, New Task.
	- **Example**: Sheng Siong reordering vegetables (straight rebuy).
- **Buying Centre Roles**: Users, Influencers, Buyers, Deciders, Gatekeepers.
	- **Example**: Polytechnic AI lab purchase involves lecturers (users), IT department (influencers), procurement (buyers).
- **8-Stage Buying Process**.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **B2C is emotional & individual**, B2B is rational & organizational.
	- **Example**: Luxury bag purchase (emotional) vs cybersecurity software purchase (rational).
- **Cultural & social factors heavily influence SG consumers**.
	- **Example**: Chinese New Year bundles, Malay modest fashion.
- **B2B buying is a team effort**—identify key decision-makers.
- **Derived demand means B2B marketers must understand end-consumer trends**.
	- **Example**: Toilet paper demand spike during pandemic affected upstream suppliers.
___
## **Lecture 5: Segmentation, Targeting & Positioning (STP)**
### **🎯 Why STP Matters**
- **Bridge between analysis and 4Ps**.
- **Answers**: Who? Which? How?
### **🧩 Segmentation Bases**
- Geographic, Demographic, Psychographic, Behavioural.
	- **Example**: Decathlon segments by sports enthusiasts (behavioral).
- **Behavioural is most powerful** (based on actual actions).
	- **Example**: Heavy users of GrabFood vs occasional users.
### **✅ Effective Segmentation Criteria**
- Measurable, Substantial, Accessible, Differentiable, Actionable.
	- **Example**: Silver segment (aging population) is substantial and accessible in SG.
### **🎯 Targeting Strategies**
- Undifferentiated, Differentiated, Concentrated, Micromarketing.
	- **Example**: Rolex uses concentrated targeting (luxury buyers).
### **📍 Differentiation & Positioning**
- **Differentiation**: How you are different (product, service, channel, people, image).
	- **Example**: Dyson = engineering excellence; Charles & Keith = affordable luxury.
- **Positioning**: How you are perceived.
	- **Example**: Apple = sophistication & innovation.
- **Perceptual Maps**: Visualize competitive positioning.
	- **Example**: Luxury vs affordable, high vs low quality.
### **📝 Positioning Statement Template**
> For [target], [brand] is the [point of difference] that [reason to believe].
**Example**: For busy professionals, Grab is the convenient ride‑hailing service that saves time through reliable on‑demand transport.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **STP is sequential and strategic**.
- **Segmentation must be actionable**.
	- **Example**: NTUC FairPrice segments by income—FairPrice Finest for higher-income neighborhoods.
- **Targeting is about choice, not just identification**.
- **Positioning is about perception, not just product features**.
	- **Example**: Tiger Beer positions as “Uncage Your Tiger” (2023) and “You Never Roar Alone” (2024) for emotional connection.
- **SG Example**: FairPrice targets different segments with FairPrice Finest vs regular outlets.
___
## **Lecture 6: Product & Customer Value**
### **🧩 Three Levels of Product**
- Core Benefit, Actual Product, Augmented Product.
	- **Example**: iPhone = Core (communication), Actual (hardware/iOS), Augmented (AppleCare, iCloud).
### **📦 Product Types**
- Consumer (Convenience, Shopping, Specialty, Unsought).
	- **Example**: Pokka drinks (convenience), Dyson hairdryer (specialty).
- Industrial (Materials, Capital, Supplies).
	- **Example**: ST Engineering purchases aircraft parts.
### **🏷️ Branding & Packaging**
- Brand Equity, Line vs Brand Extensions.
	- **Example**: Tiger Beer → Tiger Crystal (line extension); Coca-Cola → apparel (brand extension).
- Packaging: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary.
	- **Example**: Bottle (primary), cardboard box (secondary), shipping pallet (tertiary).
### **📊 Product Mix & Life Cycle**
- Width, Length, Depth, Consistency.
	- **Example**: P&G has width = 5 product lines, depth varies per brand.
- PLC Stages: Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline.
	- **Example**: Electric vehicles in early growth phase in SG.
### **🧪 Diffusion of Innovation**
- Relative Advantage, Compatibility, Complexity, Trialability, Observability.
	- **Example**: EVs are improving in compatibility and observability in SG.
### **🛠️ Services Marketing**
- **4 I’s**: Intangible, Inseparable, Variable, Perishable.
	- **Example**: Medical consultation (inseparable), empty airline seat (perishable).
- **Extended 7Ps**: + People, Process, Physical Evidence.
	- **Example**: DBS uses digital tools and strong culture (people/process) for service excellence.
- **Service-Profit Chain** (DBS Case Study).
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **Product = value delivery vehicle**.
- **Augmented product is key to differentiation**.
	- **Example**: Best Denki extended warranty adds peace of mind.
- **Services require special marketing (7Ps)**.
- **PLC helps plan strategy over time**.
	- **Example**: Maturity stage requires branding and efficiency.
- **Innovation adoption depends on perceived value, not just features**.
___
## **Lecture 7: Pricing**
### **💰 Pricing Strategies**
- **Customer Value-Based**: Premium (Apple, Bacha Coffee).
- **Good-Value**: Balanced (Decathlon, Uniqlo).
- **Cost-Based**: Cost-plus (hawker stalls adjusting for rent).
- **Competition-Based**: Matching competitors (Singtel vs StarHub).
### **📉 Price Elasticity of Demand**
- Elastic (>1), Inelastic (<1), Unitary (=1).
	- **Example**: Petrol is inelastic; bubble tea is elastic.
### **🎯 Pricing Approaches**
- New Product: Skimming vs Penetration.
	- **Example**: Foodpanda used penetration pricing on entry.
- Product Line, Optional, Captive, By-Product, Bundle Pricing.
	- **Example**: Nespresso machines ($1) with costly capsules (captive pricing).
- Adjustment Strategies: Discounts, Segmented, Psychological, Promotional, Dynamic.
	- **Example**: Shopee 11.11 sales (promotional), Grab surge pricing (dynamic).
### **⚖️ Public Policy & Ethics**
- CCCS regulations, Price Fixing, Predatory Pricing, Deceptive Pricing.
	- **Example**: Fresh chicken distributors fined for price fixing.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **Price communicates value and positioning**.
	- **Example**: LV uses prestige pricing; FairPrice uses EDLP.
- **Elasticity determines pricing power**.
- **SG consumers are price-sensitive but value-driven**.
	- **Example**: Bubble tea market is competitive; pricing must match quality perception.
- **Ethical pricing builds trust and avoids legal issues**.
___
## **Lecture 8: Distribution / Place**
### **🚚 Marketing Channels**
- **Definition**: Set of interdependent organizations.
- **Channel Levels**: Direct, Indirect, Hybrid.
	- **Example**: [Xiaomi.com](https://xiaomi.com/) (direct), Samsung → Courts (indirect).
- **Channel Flows**: Physical, Information, Ownership, Financing, Risk.
### **🏪 Channel Design & Management**
- **Design Steps**: Analyse needs → Set objectives → Identify alternatives → Evaluate.
	- **Example**: FairPrice uses hybrid distribution (wholesalers + e-commerce).
- **Distribution Intensity**: Intensive, Selective, Exclusive.
	- **Example**: Coca-Cola (intensive), Adidas (selective), Rolex (exclusive).
- **Channel Conflicts**: Horizontal & Vertical.
	- **Example**: Shopee vs FairPrice price differences cause vertical conflict.
### **🤝 Vertical vs Horizontal Marketing Systems**
- **VMS**: Corporate, Contractual, Administered.
	- **Example**: Apple stores (corporate VMS), 7-Eleven franchise (contractual VMS).
- **HMS**: Collaboration at same level.
	- **Example**: GrabMart partners with supermarkets.
### **🔄 Multichannel vs Omnichannel**
- **Multichannel**: Company-centric, independent channels.
- **Omnichannel**: Customer-centric, integrated experience.
	- **Example**: Decathlon click-and-collect (omnichannel).
### **🛍️ Retailing in Singapore**
- **Retail Mix**: Assortment, Pricing, Location, Promotion, Services, Atmospherics.
	- **Example**: IKEA uses guided pathways and workshops (atmospherics + services).
- **Technology**: Self-checkout, RFID, virtual try-ons.
	- **Example**: Watsons app virtual try-ons for beauty products.
- **Trends**: Showrooming, Webrooming, Pop-up Stores.
	- **Example**: Pop-up stores in Orchard Road test new markets.
### **📦 Wholesaling & B2B Channels**
- **Types**: Merchant Wholesalers, Brokers, Agents, Manufacturers' Operations.
	- **Example**: DKSH (FMCG distributor), Ban Choon Marketing (fresh produce).
- **Trends**: Automation, Digital B2B Platforms, JIT Logistics.
	- **Example**: [Eezee.sg](https://eezee.sg/) for online wholesale ordering.
### **🌱 Sustainability & Ethics**
- Reusable packaging, reverse logistics, fair trade, transparency.
	- **Example**: FairPrice carries Fairtrade-certified products; RedMart optimizes delivery routes with AI.
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **Channels deliver value, not just products**.
	- **Example**: RedMart adds value through bulk storage and last-mile delivery.
- **Channel design affects accessibility and competitiveness**.
	- **Example**: Long channels suit high-volume, low-margin products (e.g., rice, milk).
- **Omnichannel is customer-centric, multichannel is company-centric**.
- **SG retail is tech-integrated and experience-focused**.
	- **Example**: Jewel Changi combines retail, dining, and attractions.
- **Sustainability is a growing channel imperative**.
	- **Example**: IKEA take-back programs, Sheng Siong local sourcing.
___
## **Lecture 9: Promotion & IMC**
### **🗣️ Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC)**
- **Definition**: Coordinated, consistent messaging across all channels.
- **Why IMC**: Prevents mixed messages, improves recall, aligns teams.
	- **Example**: NTUC FairPrice "Stretch Your Dollar" campaign across app, stores, social media.
### **🛠️ Promotion Mix (5 Tools)**
- Advertising, PR, Sales Promotion, Personal Selling, Direct & Digital Marketing.
	- **Example**: Sentosa uses all 5—YouTube ads, tourism PR, island passes, corporate partnerships, TikTok.
### **📣 Communication Process**
- Sender → Encoding → Message → Decoding → Receiver → Feedback.
	- **Example**: LTA safe-riding campaign—encoding via safety icons, decoding by commuters.
- **AIDA Model**: Attention, Interest, Desire, Action.
	- **Example**: Changi Airport LED OOH (attention), Mr Coconut "fresh daily" (desire).
### **📢 Push vs Pull Strategies**
- **Push**: Through intermediaries (e.g., Nestlé negotiates shelf space with FairPrice).
- **Pull**: Direct to consumers (e.g., Milo TikTok challenges).
### **📊 Advertising & PR**
- **Advertising Types**: Informative, Persuasive, Reminder.
	- **Example**: SP Group bill explainer (informative), Yakult daily health (reminder).
- **PR**: Earned media, events, CSR.
	- **Example**: Changi Airport Jewel events, DBS Sparks campaign.
- **Evaluation**: Pre-testing (A/B tests), Post-testing.
	- **Example**: NTUC Income runs A/B tests on digital ad creatives.
### **👥 Personal Selling & Sales Promotion**
- **Personal Selling**: Relationship-based (B2C, B2B).
	- **Example**: Property agents (PropNex), insurance advisors (AIA).
- **Sales Promotion**: Short-term incentives (Consumer, Trade, Business).
	- **Example**: FairPrice 3-day deals (consumer), FMCG shelf placement fees (trade).
### **⚖️ Ethical & Regulatory Issues**
- SCAP, MAS, PDPA, Fair Dealing.
	- **Example**: Financial products must have clear disclosures (MAS).
___
### **📌 Learning Points & Takeaways**
- **IMC ensures brand consistency**.
	- **Example**: Singapore Tourism Board uses immersive ads, PR, influencers, digital retargeting.
- **Promotion mix must be integrated, not isolated**.
- **Push vs Pull depends on product and channel strategy**.
- **SG has high digital adoption → digital-first promotion is key**.
	- **Example**: Shopee Live, influencer livestreams.
- **Ethical promotion builds long-term trust**.
